Loomline — Privacy Policy

Effective 12 June 2026

The short version: Loomline collects no data. No accounts, no analytics, no ads, no tracking, no third-party SDKs. Everything the app knows stays on your device.

What we collect

Nothing. Loomline does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information — from anyone, including children. The app has no sign-up, no login, and no server of ours to send anything to.

What stays on your device

Loomline saves your puzzle progress, Daily Puzzle streak, and settings locally on your device using Apple's standard app-storage mechanisms. This data never leaves your device through Loomline, and deleting the app deletes it.

Purchases

The optional one-time unlock is processed entirely by Apple through the App Store (StoreKit). Apple handles the payment; we never receive your name, payment details, or any other personal information from the transaction. Apple's handling of that data is described in Apple's Privacy Policy.

Sharing features

When you choose to share a solved-puzzle card or Daily Puzzle result, the image or text is generated on your device and handed to the iOS share sheet. You choose where it goes; Loomline sends nothing anywhere on its own.

Analytics and tracking

There are none. No analytics frameworks, no advertising identifiers, no tracking of any kind. The app's privacy manifest declares no data collection and no tracking.

Children

Because Loomline collects no data at all, no data is collected from children.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of Loomline ever changes any of the above (for example, adding optional iCloud progress sync), this policy will be updated here before that version ships, with the effective date revised.
